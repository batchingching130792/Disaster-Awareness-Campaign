What is an Earthquake?

An earthquake is the sudden, violent shaking of the ground caused by volcanic activity or the shifting of tectonic plates
underneath the Earth's surface due to the sudden release of accumulated energy in waves that travel through the
earth's crust, potentially causing great destruction without warning.

The Philippines is along the Pacific Ring of Fire, also called the Circum-Pacific Belt,
a path on the ocean where active volcanoes lie; and earthquakes frequently happen.

Effects of Earthquakes

▶ Ground Displacement and Surface Rupture

- can affect large land areas and produce severe damage to structures, roads, railways and pipelines

▶ Landslides

- may result in falling rocks and debris
- can cause blockage of roads and disrupt utility lines.

▶ Liquefaction

- can turn loose soil into liquid during a tremor
- can undermine structures and foundations causing them to sink into the ground, collapse, or dissolve

▶ Tsunamis

- an earthquake generated within the Pacific Ocean Floor will generate a tsunami, which is a series of
very long waves which can travel from the ocean floor to the surface, dangerous to human health, property, and infrastructure

▶ Fires

- when electrical and gas lines are dislodged due to the earth's shaking,
it can potentially cause a spark that could eventually become huge flames

Earthquake Safety 101 - What Should I Do Before, During, and After an Earthquake?

Before an Earthquake

  • PRACTICE the Drop, Cover, and Hold On safety drill with your relatives.

  • DEVELOP an emergency earthquake plan with your family.

  • DECIDE on a safe meeting place if separated; an open area free from trees,
    telephone poles, and buildings.

  • KNOW every spot in your households; those that are safe, and those that are danger spots.

  • PREPARE an emergency supply kit that includes enough food and water
    for several days, flashlights, a radio, a fire extinguisher, and a whistle.

  • SECURE hazaardous equipment (heavy furniture, mirrors, hanging objects, and flammables).
