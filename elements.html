Earthquake Safety 101 - What Should I Do Before, During, and After an Earthquake?

    Before an Earthquake Strikes...

      • PRACTICE the Drop, Cover, and Hold On safety drill with your relatives.

      • DEVELOP an emergency earthquake plan with your family.

      • DECIDE on a safe meeting place if separated; an open area free from trees,
        telephone poles, and buildings.

      • KNOW every spot in your households; those that are safe, and those that are danger spots.

      • PREPARE an emergency supply kit that includes enough food and water
        for several days, flashlights, a radio, a fire extinguisher, and a whistle.

      • SECURE hazardous equipment (heavy furniture, mirrors, hanging objects, and flammables).

      • KEEP a list of emergency contact numbers.

During an Earthquake...

      • STAY calm and don't panic.

      • PERFORM the Drop, Cover, and Hold On tactic.

      • REMAIN where you are until the shaking stops.

      • THINK out your plan of action first, then move.

      • EVACUATE to the safest and nearest shelter in your area.

            • IF INSIDE...

              - stay there and crawl under heavy funiture (desks, tables, chairs, etc.)
              - steer clear of windows, doors, and shelves with heavy objects

            IF OUTSIDE...

              - go to an open area away from anything tall that might collapse
              (buildings, towers, tall trees, power lines, etc.)

            IF YOU'RE IN A VEHICLE...

              - pull over and stop driving as soon as possible
              - stay inside until the shaking stops

      To Stay Safe After an Earthquake...

        • EXPECT aftershocks to follow the main shock of an earthquake.

            • IF YOU'RE IN A DAMAGED BUILDING...

            - go outside and quickly move away from the building

            IF YOU ARE TRAPPED...

            - cover your mouth, nose, and eyes against dust and for protection
            - send a text or make some noise to alert the rescuers to your location
            - instead of shouting, use a whistle

            IF YOU'RE IN AN AREA THAT MAY EXPERIENCE TSUNAMIS...

            - go inland or to higher ground immediately after the shaking stops
            - avoid contact with floodwaters that can contain chemicals, sewage, and debris

      • PREPARE to Drop, Cover, and Hold if you feel an afterschock coming.

      • WASH your hands with soap and water or hand sanitizer containing at least 60 percent alcohol.

      • GATHER people for roll call.

      • MAKE SURE everyone has evacuated safely and remember to check up on every person
        within the vicinity, especially those injured.

          IF THERE ARE MISSING PEOPLE...

          - seek help from the disaster management team and identify the missing victims

      • PROVIDE first-aid if necessary and help injurd victims.

      • COOPERATE with public safety officials.

      • BEWARE of fallen debris and stay out of damaged facilities.

      • TURN ON the radio and listen for news.

      • CONSERVE your phone's battery; use only for emergency purposes.

      • WEAR protective gear.

      • INSPECT for hazards in your area (gas leaks, chemical spills, damaged water and electric lines, etc.).
